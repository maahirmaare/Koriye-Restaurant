our speciality

Canjeero

A common breakfast dish, Canjeero is made from flour (or corn flour) and yeast. The batter ferments overnight and is then cooked into a soft, circular pancake. It’s typically served with sesame oil and sugar or dallac bilaash, a tomato sauce with meat

Muufo

Muufo is a corn flour flatbread that doesn’t rise during baking. It’s baked in a traditional Somali clay oven called a tinaar. Enjoy it with suqaar (meat sauce), soup, sesame oil, and banana slices

Canbulo Iyo Bun (Beans and Coffee Berries)

Canbulo Iyo Bun (Beans and Coffee Berries): This dish combines beans and coffee berries. Beans are a staple, and coffee is an integral part of Somali culture. The coastal regions also offer delicious seafood

isku karis

pasta and rice is a favorite in Somali cuisine due to Italy’s historical influence. The popular pasta dish includes meatballs, tomatoes, potato slices, garlic, onion, and xawaaji (a Somali spice mixture).
